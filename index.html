Deadline UI/GUI

Working on Deadline, I was in charge of UI creation. My plan with UI was to create something that would be sleek and modern to match the environment stylings. The aim of the UI was to show as much information as possible for the player without sacrificing valuable screen space. The resulting style consisted of sloping angles and sharply defined icons with vibrant colours to differentiate both players UI pieces.

Deadline Game Environment

My job during Deadline was the creation of the Environment and all assets that fill it. The goal of the environment was to create a deep palette of colours with warm neon colours. Vibrant neon glow and a staccato of shadows to emphasize the bloom and emmisive effects that comprise the environment.

Assets were made to be low poly to give breathing room for the AI and the sheer number of enemies, we needed to make sure that the framerate was as high as possible, and balance that with making the assets look as good as possible. Each segment of the map followed a slightly different style, from the maintenance halls to the catwalks that hung over the storage facility.

Candy Slinger

During this project I spent my time working on the game's UI and sprite assets. The colours were made to be warm and inviting while the soft outlines, puffy colouring, and glossy effects assisted in making the game simple and easy to look at. The stylings of the game revolve around candy, because of this all sprites made relate to candy in some form. For example, speed boosts are peppermint candy while the hammers are lollipops.
